## When you need this

Schroedinger Sync is offered publicly under the [GNU AGPLv3](LICENSE). The AGPLv3
is a strong, *network*-copyleft licence: if you convey the software — or run a
modified version as a network/SaaS service that others interact with — you must
make your corresponding source code available under the AGPLv3 as well.

That is exactly right for individuals and for open projects. It does **not** fit an
organisation that wants to:

- **embed** Schroedinger Sync (or a derivative) inside a **closed-source** product;
- **offer it as part of a SaaS / hosted service** without releasing that service's
  source;
- **redistribute** it under terms other than the AGPLv3; or
- obtain **warranty, indemnity, or support** terms that the free AS-IS edition
  deliberately does not provide.

For any of those, you need a **commercial licence** — a separate, private agreement
that grants the right to use the code *without* the AGPLv3's copyleft obligations.

## What the commercial licence grants (intended)

- The right to use, modify, and distribute Schroedinger Sync inside a proprietary,
  closed-source, or SaaS product, without triggering AGPLv3 source-disclosure.
- Negotiable warranty / support / indemnity terms.
- Optional access to the proprietary **Enterprise layer** (multi-tenant, SSO/LDAP,
  audit logging, team sync, compliance tooling) — sold separately from the open core.

## How this is possible

Every line of Schroedinger Sync is owned by a single rights-holder, and every
external contribution is governed by the [Contributor License Agreement](CLA.md).
That consolidation of rights is what allows the same code to be offered under *both*
the AGPLv3 and a commercial licence. It is the whole reason the CLA is mandatory.
